Chaliapin Steak Don from Food Wars

Japanese steak dish made famous from the anime Food Wars

Based on the preferences of Russian opera singer Feodor Chaliapan and created by Japanese chef Fukuo Tsutsui in 1936

This steak dish is paired with rice seasoned with shiso and umeboshi, and topped with caramelized onions and a red wine sauce

Ingredients

Steak and Rice

  • 2 Thick Steaks, preferably sirloin or something similar
  • 3 White onions
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 1tbsp Oil or beef fat (choose an oil that is neutral and with a high smoke point)
  • 300g Cooked short grain white rice
  • 3 Umeboshi pickled plums
  • 4 Shiso leaves or Ooba leaves
  • 2 tbsp Green Onion chopped for garnish

Sauce

  • 1 tbsp Unsalted butter
  • 5 tbsp Red wine
  • 1 tbsp Soy Sauce
  • Slurry - 1 tbsp Water +1 tsp of corn starch

Cooking Instructions (based on Chaliapan Steak Don from Sudachi Recipes

Prepping Steak

  1. Finely chop or greate 1 onion into a rough paste (still chunky)
  2. Tendarize Steak by wrapping in sling film or butcher paper and using a rolling pin or meat tenderiser preferably if you have, flatten to roughly 3 quarters of an inch
  3. Score both sides of the steak lightly with diaganol cuts across
  4. Place steak in a pan, plate or lid with roughly half the grated onions on bottom and cover with the rest
  5. Leave steak covered somewhere room temperature for 30 minutes (to get a better char)

Sauteed Onions

  1. Finely chop remaining onions
  2. Place 1 tbsp of butter in frying pan over medium heat
  3. After butter is melted place the chooped onions into the pan, cook until light golden brown
  4. Once light golden brown add salt and pepper to taste, stir and finish cooking to Golden Brown
  5. Post cooking place aside

Cooking Steak

  1. Remove onion from steak, and then wash briefly and pat dry with a paper towl
  2. Season both sides with salt and pepper to taste
  3. heat pan to high, once it begins to smoke add oil or fat (whichever you chose to use)
  4. Add steak to pan and cook 1 minute to 30 seconds per side depending on oven temp and desired doneness, looking for slight char on both sides
  5. Once cooked to your prefered doneness (medium rare for authenticity) remove from pan and leave to rest

Sauce

  1. Using the same pan as the steaks melt 1 tbsp of butter on medium heat
  2. After completely melted and fragrent, add soy sauce
  3. Once Soy Sauce becomes fragrent add the red wine
  4. Add any juices that have leaked from the steak
  5. Cook for a few minutes so the alcholo burns off, should be bubbling
  6. Create slurry by mixing water and corn starch and then add to the sauce
  7. Mix till thickened, should be able to drag spoon across and see the bottom of the pan

Ume Rice

  1. Cut shiso leaves into thin leaves
  2. De-sead the Umeboshi and dice finely to almost a paste
  3. Add cooked rice to a large bowl and add the umeboshi and shiso leaves and mix

Plating

  1. Split rice into 2 bowls
  2. Cut steaks to thin strips and place ontop of rice (ideally for best looks in strips length wise so it looks like a steak flower almost)
  3. Add Sauteed onions on top and drizzle with steak sauce (do the suace first for best aesthetics)
  4. Garnish with Green Onions
